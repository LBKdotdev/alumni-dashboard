What is the NPI Registry? The National Provider Identifier (NPI) is a unique 10-digit number assigned to every practicing physician in the US — including every DO. It's maintained by CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) and released publicly under the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA). Your team likely already works with NPI data for credentialing and GME. This approach flips that same database around — instead of verifying a provider, you cross-reference graduation records to locate alumni. The NPPES API is free, requires no authentication, and is updated monthly.
Privacy & Opt-Out: All data shown comes from public federal records. Alumni who prefer not to be contacted can opt out at any time — the system includes a one-click removal. No social media scraping, no third-party data brokers. Just public records + voluntary participation.
How It Works
🎓
Automated Discovery
Cross-reference graduation records with the NPI Registry API. An estimated 75–85% of alumni can be matched automatically with practice mailing addresses and specialties. Match rate validated in Phase 1.
🔄
Continuous Updates
Monthly sync with NPPES data. When alumni move practices, update specialties, or earn new credentials — the system knows. Your data never goes stale.
🔒
Privacy First
Core data from the NPI Registry (federal, FOIA-released). Additional profile enrichment is voluntary opt-in only. FERPA-compliant. No social media scraping. One-click opt-out for any alumni who prefers not to participate.

Data Sources — Tiered by Access Level

NPPES / NPI Registry
AUTOMATED
Federal database (CMS). FOIA-disclosed. Every practicing physician — including DOs. Name, specialty taxonomy, practice mailing address, credentials. Free API, no auth required. Updated monthly.
State Medical Boards
VERIFICATION
CA & OR boards. License status, practice location. Public record for individual lookups. Used to verify and supplement NPI data.
Doximity (Voluntary Opt-In)
ALUMNI-DRIVEN
Alumni can self-link their Doximity profiles to enrich their record with residency, fellowship, and research data. No automated collection — their TOS prohibits scraping.
PubMed / Google Scholar
PUBLIC
Published research with institutional affiliations. Identifies research-active alumni and potential collaborators. Fully public data.
