# Audio Coaching Session — Scotty Prepares for Dr. Lisa Warren

Generate an audio overview that coaches Scotty through his upcoming meeting. This is a coaching session, not a summary. Speak directly to Scotty in second person ("you") throughout.

**Tone:** Warm, direct, confident — like a trusted chief of staff the night before. A musician's rehearsal — he knows the song, you're helping him feel the groove so it's effortless live. Not a TED talk. Not consultant-speak. A friend helping a friend prepare.

---

## Audio Learning Instructions (Critical)

Scotty is an audio learner. Repetition is how things stick.

When you reach a key phrase he needs word-for-word: **SLOW DOWN, say it clearly, pause, then say it a second time.** Briefly explain why it matters after the repetition. Let important moments breathe.

**Phrases that MUST be repeated twice:**
- Her North Star quote
- His opening question for Lisa
- The discovery question about NPI/PubMed automation
- "How did they even know? My school still sees me."
- "That's one person. Now multiply across 2,600 graduates. The system watches. Your team connects."
- "I'd rather build something small that actually works than promise something big that doesn't."

---

## Coaching Flow (in this order)

1. **Ground him.** 25-year friendship. Bass player energy — right note, right time, leave space.
2. **Three anchors.** Her North Star (repeat). His opening question (repeat). His posture: Listen, Reflect, Elevate.
3. **What he knows but holds.** Walk through the institutional landscape from the source docs. Coach him to HOLD this knowledge — it sharpens his questions, it never becomes a presentation.
4. **Conversation flow — six planks.** Extra time on Plank 2 (she might not talk about alumni first — follow wherever she goes) and Plank 4 (where the relationship engine enters through the humble delivery).
5. **The relationship engine delivery — SPEND THE MOST TIME HERE.** Walk through the seven-step humble delivery sequence slowly. Coach the Dr. Park story as it sounds spoken aloud. Emphasize: the data sources are not novel — the value is the orchestration. After delivering, sit. Don't fill the silence.
6. **If they already have something.** Coach the humble pivot.
7. **Back pocket scenarios.** Coach ready responses for: finding vs. meaning, data reliability, voluntary opt-in, giving reframe, and the elevator sentence Lisa can give her provost.
8. **This is personal for her.** She IS the alumni network — Class of 2001. If she gets emotional, don't rush past it. Mirror, don't solve.
9. **If it goes really well.** Coach the three asks.
10. **Rob Monday preview.** Discovery first — ask before showing the dashboard. Three scenarios based on Rob's answer.
11. **Close — The One Thing.** You don't need to be impressive. You need to be present. Be yourself. The rest follows.

---

## What This Should NOT Sound Like
- Not a document summary — coach him through it
- Not jargon — he hates it
- Never rushed on the relationship engine delivery or the emotional coaching about Lisa
