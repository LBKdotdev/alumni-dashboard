# Meaningful Engagement — Discovery Questions
## For Dr. Lisa Warren & Team

*"I look forward to empowering our community to discover and share their talents, fostering meaningful engagement." — Dr. Lisa Warren, on becoming Dean*

*These questions are built around that standard. The goal isn't a better database or a fancier dashboard — it's a system that creates meaningful engagement between COMP and the people who carry its mission into the world.*

---

## 0. The Definition — Ask This Early

**0a.** When you think about the alumni relationship developer — is that a role you're looking to fill, a system you want to build, or something bigger?

*Why this comes first:* This one question could completely change the direction of the conversation. If she's hiring for a person, the conversation is different than if she's building a platform. Better to know in the first five minutes than to spend thirty pitching the wrong thing.

---

## 1. The Current Landscape

**1a.** When a COMP graduate walks across the stage, what happens to their record? Where does it go — a CRM, a spreadsheet, the registrar's office? Who owns it after commencement?

**1b.** If you needed to contact every alumnus from the Class of 2018 tomorrow, how would your team go about it? What would that process actually look like?

**1c.** How much of your current alumni data would you trust? If you had to guess — what percentage of contact information is still accurate?

**1d.** Is there an existing alumni database or CRM in use? If so, what's working about it — and where does it fall short?

---

## 2. The Two Campuses

**2a.** Do Pomona and COMP-Northwest alumni feel like one community or two separate ones? Is there any cross-campus connection happening today?

**2b.** Are there differences in how connected each campus feels to its alumni? Does one have a stronger relationship than the other — and if so, why?

**2c.** Would the engagement approach need to look different for each campus, or should it be unified with the same tools and touchpoints?

---

## 3. What Meaningful Engagement Looks Like

**3a.** If this alumni engagement effort is working exactly the way you want it 12 months from now — what's different? What can you do that you can't do today?

**3b.** You've talked about fostering meaningful engagement — what does that actually look like in practice? Is it alumni mentoring students? Showing up at events? Contributing to the program? All of the above?

**3c.** What would make an alumnus *feel seen* by the school — not just contacted, but actually valued?

**3d.** Are there specific metrics the university or COCA expects to see? Things like residency placement rates, board pass rates, alumni survey response rates, donor participation?

**3e.** When accreditation reporting comes around, how much of the graduate outcomes data is manual work today? Is that a pain point, or is it manageable?

**3f.** Now that we've talked about what meaningful engagement looks like — if you had to rank these four, which matters most right now?
  - Knowing where alumni are and what they're doing
  - Building a mentorship pipeline between alumni and students
  - Improving donor participation and fundraising
  - Streamlining accreditation and outcomes reporting

---

## 4. Outreach & Connection

**4a.** When alumni hear from the school today, what does that typically look like? Email newsletters, event invitations, phone calls, direct mail?

**4b.** What kind of response do you get? Is there a sense of whether alumni *want* to be connected — or does it feel like reaching into a void?

**4c.** Have there been any outreach moments that felt genuinely meaningful — where an alumnus re-engaged and it turned into something real? What made that happen?

**4d.** On the flip side — has anything fallen flat? What didn't land, and do you have a sense of why?

**4e.** When an alumnus *does* re-engage — volunteers to mentor, shows up at an event, makes a gift — is there a process that keeps that relationship warm? Or does it tend to go quiet again?

**4f.** What would make an alumnus *feel seen* by the school — not just contacted, but actually valued?

---

## 5. Mentorship

**5a.** Is there a formal mentorship program connecting alumni to current students today? If so, how does matching work — by specialty, location, interest?

**5b.** Do students ask for mentors in specific specialties and you can't find them? Where are the gaps?

**5c.** What would make it easy for an alumnus to say yes to mentoring? What's the lowest-friction version of that ask?

**5d.** Are there alumni who'd be great mentors but don't know the school is looking — or haven't been asked?

**5e.** Is mentorship where you see the deepest form of meaningful engagement — alumni sharing their talents with the next generation? Or does it live somewhere else?

---

## 6. Data, Privacy & Compliance

**6a.** How does the university currently define its FERPA directory information? Specifically — does the directory information designation carry over to alumni, and what fields does it include?

**6b.** Is there a compliance team or office that would need to review any alumni data initiative before it launches? How early should they be involved?

**6c.** How comfortable is the institution with using public federal data sources (like NPI/NPPES and state medical board records) to locate and verify alumni practice information?

**6d.** Would alumni opt-in be a hard requirement for any outreach — or can the university initiate contact using directory information and public records?

**6e.** Has the university had any past concerns or incidents around alumni data that would shape how cautiously we'd need to move?

---

## 7. Team & Tools

**7a.** Who on your team would be the day-to-day person on this — is there someone dedicated to alumni engagement, or would it fold into existing responsibilities?

**7b.** Are there existing tools or platforms the university is committed to (Salesforce, Raiser's Edge, Slate, etc.) that any new system would need to work alongside?

**7c.** What's the appetite for a phased approach — start small with one class year, validate, then expand — versus building the full picture all at once?

---

## 8. The Bigger Picture

**8a.** Beyond alumni engagement, are there other areas where better data and workflows could help — admissions outreach, faculty development, clinical placement coordination?

**8b.** Is this initiative coming from your office specifically, or is there broader institutional support from advancement, the provost, or the board?

**8c.** What would make you feel confident that this is headed in the right direction after the first 30 days?

---

*These questions are meant to be used conversationally — not read off a list. Pull the right thread at the right moment. The goal is to leave the conversation knowing exactly what to build first, why it matters, and what "empowering our community to discover and share their talents, fostering meaningful engagement" looks like through her eyes — in her own words, not yours.*
