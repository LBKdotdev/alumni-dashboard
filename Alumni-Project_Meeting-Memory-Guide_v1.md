# Meeting Memory Guide — Dr. Lisa Warren
## Learn It. Own It. Forget the Notes.

*Based on Jim Kwik's principles: Association, Emotion, Location, Chunking. Don't memorize words — memorize the feeling and the flow. Your brain remembers stories, not lists.*

---

## 1. THE ONE THING

If you forget everything else, remember this:

**Listen for what "meaningful engagement" means to HER.**

That's it. That's the whole meeting. Everything else is just how you get there.

---

## 2. THE FLOW — Six Moves

Think of the conversation like a song with six parts. You already know how songs work.

**Memory anchor: W-L-M-S-A-C** → "**W**e **L**isten, **M**irror, **S**hare, **A**sk, **C**lose"

| # | Move | Time | What You're Doing | The One Line |
|---|------|------|-------------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Warm** | 2–3 min | Connect through Rob. Be human. | *"Rob's been great connecting us — I've been looking forward to this."* |
| 2 | **Listen** | 10–15 min | Let her talk. Sit with silence. | *"I'd love to hear what's on your mind — what's the vision?"* |
| 3 | **Mirror** | 2–3 min | Prove you heard her. Use her words. | *"So meaningful engagement for COMP looks like [her words]."* |
| 4 | **Share** | 5–8 min | Think out loud. Not a pitch. | *"What you're describing is a meaningful engagement problem."* |
| 5 | **Ask** | 2–3 min | Let her pick where to start. | *"What's the first thing that would move the needle?"* |
| 6 | **Close** | Natural | Warm. Confirm next step. | *"I'll follow up with [whatever you agreed to]."* |

**Jim Kwik technique:** Walk through your house. Place each move in a room.
- Front door = **Warm** (you're greeting her)
- Living room = **Listen** (you sit down and she talks)
- Kitchen = **Mirror** (you serve back what she gave you)
- Office = **Share** (you think together)
- Hallway = **Ask** (which door do we open?)
- Back door = **Close** (you leave with clarity)

Practice walking through your house tonight. Each room triggers the next move.

---

## 3. THE FIRST QUESTION THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING

Before anything else, within the first 5 minutes:

> **"When you think about the alumni relationship developer — is that a role you're looking to fill, a system you want to build, or something bigger?"**

**Why it matters:** If she says "a role" — the conversation is about a job, not a system. If she says "a system" — you're on track. If she says "something bigger" — listen harder. This question saves you from spending 30 minutes pitching the wrong thing.

**Memory anchor:** Think of it as **the fork in the road.** Before you can drive anywhere, you need to know which road you're on.

---

## 4. THE FIVE QUESTIONS — Your A-List

You don't need 40 questions. You need five. Know these cold.

**Memory anchor: D-V-S-F-T** → "**D**efine **V**ision, **S**een, **F**eel, **T**alent"

| # | Keyword | The Question | What You Learn |
|---|---------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Define** | "What does meaningful engagement actually look like in practice?" | Her definition — this IS the brief |
| 2 | **Vision** | "If this is working perfectly a year from now — what's different?" | Her success criteria |
| 3 | **Seen** | "What would make an alumnus feel *seen* — not just contacted?" | Her values — the emotional core |
| 4 | **Feel** | "When alumni hear from the school, what do you want that to feel like?" | The tone for everything you build |
| 5 | **Talent** | "Is mentorship where the deepest engagement lives — alumni sharing their talents?" | Connects to her own quote |

**Jim Kwik technique:** Visualize five fingers on your hand.
- Thumb = **Define** (thumbs up = "define it for me")
- Index = **Vision** (pointing forward = "where are we going?")
- Middle = **Seen** (the tallest = "standing out, being noticed")
- Ring = **Feel** (wedding ring = "emotional connection")
- Pinky = **Talent** (smallest but strongest grip = "sharing what they have")

Look at your hand before the meeting. Each finger is a question.

## 5. ACRONYMS & BUZZWORDS — The Ones That Matter

You don't need to memorize all of them. You need to *recognize* them when she says them and know what to do.

### The Big Three She'll Use

| Term | What It Is | If She Says It |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **COCA** | Commission on Osteopathic College Accreditation. Her accrediting body. | Don't explain it back to her. She lives it. Just nod and ask: "How much of that reporting is manual right now?" |
| **GME** | Graduate Medical Education. Residency programs. Her career. | She built her reputation here — 5% above national benchmarks for 7 years. Reference it with respect, not flattery. |
| **NPPES** | National Plan and Provider Enumeration System. The NPI database. | If she mentions it, she already knows. Go straight to: "The question is what you do with that data after you find them." |

### The Ones You Use — Naturally, Not Forced

**Memory anchor: Think of them as tools in a belt, not words on a page.**

| Term | When to Drop It | How It Sounds |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| **Signal-based outreach** | When she talks about low response rates | *"That's usually a timing problem. Signal-based outreach reaches people when they're already thinking about the school."* |
| **Workflow orchestration** | When she describes a manual process | *"That's a workflow orchestration problem — each step should trigger the next."* |
| **Decision support** | If she's skeptical about AI | *"This is decision support — your team still makes every call, but with a complete picture."* |
| **Operational leverage** | When she talks about limited staff | *"One person can do what used to take five — not by working harder, but because the system handles the repetitive parts."* |
| **Meaningful engagement** | Mirror her own words | *"The real metric is meaningful engagement — are alumni actually connecting, mentoring, showing up?"* |
| **Phase-gated approach** | When discussing risk or commitment | *"Each phase earns the next. No long-term commitment until you've seen results."* |
| **Time to value** | When she asks about timeline | *"30 days to a working prototype. 60 days to first outreach."* |
| **Institutional memory** | When she mentions team turnover | *"The system holds the memory so the program outlives any one person."* |
| **Consent architecture** | When compliance comes up | *"We'd design that from day one. Opt-in, opt-out, data provenance. Not bolted on later."* |
| **Caveat** | When making any projection | *"I want to caveat this — these are working estimates. Real numbers come from Phase 1."* |

**Jim Kwik technique:** Don't memorize the list. Memorize the *trigger.* When she says "we don't have enough people," your brain should fire "operational leverage" automatically. Practice by reading the left column (the trigger) and covering the right column. Can you recall the term? That's the drill.

## 6. WORDS TO NEVER SAY

**Memory anchor: The Banned Band** — imagine a rock band that's terrible. They play these songs. You never want to hear them.

| Banned Word | Why It's Banned |
|-------------|----------------|
| AI-powered | Buzzy. Lead with what it does. |
| Machine learning | She doesn't care how. She cares what. |
| Disruptive | Overused. Sounds like a pitch deck. |
| Synergy | Just... never. |
| Leverage AI | "Leverage" alone is fine. Adding "AI" makes it a red flag. |
| Digital transformation | Sounds like a $500K PowerPoint. |
| ROI | Vendor language. Say "impact" or "value." |

## 8. IF IT GOES SIDEWAYS — Three Saves

You only need three. Each one is a redirect, not a panic move.

| Scenario | The Save |
|----------|---------|
| **She already has a vendor** | *"What's working well? Where are the gaps?"* — You complement, not compete. |
| **It's a job, not a system** | *"What would make the person in that role most effective? What tools would they need?"* — You become the enabler. |
| **Someone unexpected is in the room** | Follow Lisa's lead. Be more precise with data claims. Don't change your substance — adjust your tone. |

## 9. VIDEO CALL ADVANTAGES

This is Zoom or Teams — not a conference room. That changes a few things in your favor.

**Your secret weapon: notes on screen.** You can have your five questions, Lisa's quote, and the fork question open in a text file on your screen. She can't see them. Don't read from them — glance when you need an anchor. This is the one advantage video has over in-person. Use it.

**Granola runs silently.** No bot joins the call. No "this meeting is being recorded" popup. It captures from your device. One less thing to worry about.

**Screen share for the dashboard.** If the moment is right, the share is instant — no walking to a projector, no "let me pull this up." Just:

> *"I actually mocked something up — want me to share my screen for a second?"*

**When you share:** Have the dashboard open and ready in a browser tab BEFORE the call starts. Don't fumble with tabs on camera.

Five tabs, in order: **Overview → COCA → Mentorship → Locator → Roadmap**

Skip Donor Pipeline and Alumni Records. Those are operational. This is a vision conversation.

**The one thing to say about every tab:** *"Everything in here is sample data — just to show what's possible."*

**Stop sharing as soon as the conversation shifts back to her.** Don't let the dashboard become the meeting. The dashboard supports the conversation — it doesn't replace it. The moment she starts talking about her vision again, stop the share and go back to face-to-face. That signals: *"I care more about what you're saying than what I built."*

**Camera and presence:** Look at the camera when she's talking, not at her video feed. That's how you make eye contact on Zoom. Nod. Don't multitask. She'll feel it if you drift.

## 10. AFTER THE MEETING — The First Three Actions

| When | What | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **Immediately** | Write down her definition of meaningful engagement in her exact words | This becomes the brief for everything |
| **Within 24 hours** | Send a warm follow-up email mirroring her language | Sets you apart from everyone else |
| **Within 1 week** | Deliver a working proof of concept based on what she said matters most | Your superpower — you don't just talk, you build |

## THE NIGHT-BEFORE DRILL

**15 minutes. That's all you need.**

1. **Walk your house** (2 min) — Front door through back door. Six rooms, six moves.
2. **Look at your hand** (2 min) — Five fingers, five questions. Say them out loud.
3. **Read the triggers** (5 min) — Left column of the vocabulary table. Cover the right. Can you recall the term?
4. **Say the fork question out loud** (1 min) — "When you think about the alumni relationship developer..."
5. **Say Lisa's quote out loud** (1 min) — "Empowering our community to discover and share their talents, fostering meaningful engagement."
6. **Close your eyes and picture the meeting going well** (4 min) — Jim Kwik calls this visualization. See yourself on camera, relaxed, looking into the lens while she talks. See her face light up when you mirror her words back. See yourself sharing the dashboard — her leaning in. See yourself stopping the share because the conversation matters more than the demo. Feel what that feels like.

**Pre-call checklist:**
- Dashboard open in a browser tab, ready to share
- Notes file with your 5 questions + Lisa's quote + the fork question on screen (not visible to her)
- Granola running
- Water nearby
- Phone on silent, notifications off
- Good lighting on your face

You're not memorizing a script. You're programming your subconscious to show up ready.
