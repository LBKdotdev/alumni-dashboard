### COCA — What We Know

**What COCA is:** Commission on Osteopathic College Accreditation, part of the American Osteopathic Association. The accrediting body for all DO programs.

**How accreditation works:**
- Comprehensive site visits happen on long cycles (highest status = 10 years)
- BUT: there's continuous reporting in between — annual reports, mid-cycle reports, progress reporting
- Schools must publicly list average match rates for the last four academic years
- Graduate outcomes data (residency placement, board pass rates, career trajectories) needs to be maintained continuously
- **The pain isn't a single event — it's constant data readiness**
- Lisa deals with COCA daily. Don't explain it to her. Reference it like a peer.

**Current COCA framing for our project:**
- The system doesn't help you "prepare for a visit" — it makes the data always current
- Reports aren't something you build — they're something you pull
- The difference between manual and automated isn't about the big event, it's about the ongoing grind
- A small-but-mighty team can't afford to spend hours every cycle rebuilding reports from scratch

## SECTION 4.5: THE CROWN JEWEL — The Relationship Engine

### What It Is

Not "signal-based monitoring." That's how it works. What it IS is a **relationship engine** — a system that watches, notices, enriches, and creates the reason to reach out so that every graduate feels like the school still sees them.

Lisa built the pipeline that gets students into residency — 100% placement. But the moment they walk off that stage, the continuum breaks. The relationship engine is what closes that loop.

### Why It Matters Here Specifically

The concept of trigger-based workflows exists in every CRM. This is not a new invention. What IS new is the **domain fit**. Every practicing physician has an NPI number maintained by the federal government because billing depends on it. Every researcher shows up in PubMed. Every licensed doctor is in a state board database. The medical profession is the most trackable alumni population in higher education — and almost nobody in the osteopathic medical school space is taking advantage of it systematically.

The data infrastructure for DO graduates is uniquely suited for this. A liberal arts college can't do it — where do you find career signals for an English major? But COMP's alumni leave a public, maintained, continuously updated trail. The relationship engine turns that trail into meaningful engagement.

### The Four Layers

**Layer 1 — Detection: The system notices something changed.**
- Practice move (NPI registry, monthly updates — billing forces accuracy)
- New publication (PubMed, daily updates)
- New credential or fellowship (NPI specialty codes)
- Engagement drop (no touchpoint in 24+ months)
- Class reunion approaching (calendar-driven)
- New graduate entering the network (Match Day)
- Goal: Detect signals for 75-85% of alumni with active clinical practice within 30 days of the change.

**Layer 2 — Context: The system enriches every signal.**
- Who is this person? Class year, campus, specialty, engagement history
- Are there students who match their specialty and location?
- Is there a mentor gap in their area?
- What's their last touchpoint? How long since they heard from COMP?
- Goal: Every signal arrives to the coordinator with full context. Zero additional research needed.

**Layer 3 — Suggested Action: The system recommends what to do.**
- Not just "something changed" but "here's a draft outreach, here's why now is the right moment, here's the student match if applicable"
- The coordinator's job: personalize one line and approve — not figure out what to do from scratch
- Goal: 80% of signals come with a ready-to-review action.

**Layer 4 — The Loop: Every response becomes a new signal.**
- Alumni says yes to mentoring → enters mentorship pipeline
- Alumni attends Oregon reunion → engagement score increases
- Alumni doesn't respond → system notes it, adjusts timing for next outreach
- Nothing falls through the cracks because the engine remembers everything
- Goal: Every alumni interaction feeds back, creating institutional memory that compounds over time.

### The 0.01% Example — Dr. Park

System detects Dr. James Park, DO '19, moved from Riverside, CA to Portland, OR. 

The engine doesn't just flag the move. It:
- Recognizes this as a **COMP-NW engagement opportunity** because he's now in Oregon
- Cross-references against current 3rd-year students at Lebanon campus who listed his specialty as their interest
- Checks his engagement history — hasn't heard from COMP in three years
- Drafts a note that acknowledges the move, congratulates him, mentions the Lebanon campus is growing, and asks if he'd be open to a conversation with a student
- Places it in the coordinator's morning queue with full context

Coordinator reviews, personalizes one line, sends.

Dr. Park thinks: "How did they even know? My school still sees me."

Six months later he's mentoring two students and shows up at the Oregon reunion.

**That's not a feature. That's a relationship engine.**

### How It Connects to Everything Else

The relationship engine isn't one tab in the dashboard. It's the spine:
- **COCA reporting** = signals being captured and organized automatically — reports stay current because the system is always watching
- **Mentorship matching** = a signal (student need) matched against another signal (alumni availability, specialty, location)
- **Alumni reconnection** = a signal (engagement drop) triggering an action
- **The "my school still sees me" moment** = a signal (publication, practice move, credential) turned into a personal touchpoint
- **Donor cultivation** = engagement signals building toward giving readiness over time

Every tab is a different view of what the relationship engine produces.

## SECTION 10: COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS (Unchanged — Hard Boundaries)

### FERPA
- Student education records are federally protected
- University can release "directory information" as they define it — confirm WesternU's specific definition
- Anything beyond directory info requires alumni opt-in

### Data Tiers
- Tier 1 (Public, automated): NPPES/NPI Registry, PubMed — free, FOIA-released, no consent needed
- Tier 2 (Public, use with care): State medical boards — individual lookups OK, no bulk scraping
- Tier 3 (Voluntary only): Doximity, LinkedIn — TOS prohibits scraping, alumni self-link only

### Consent Architecture
- Opt-out available in every outreach, one click, no friction
- Every data point carries provenance (source, date, confidence)
- Alumni who opt out removed from all outreach and matching
- Anonymized records retained only for aggregate accreditation reporting

### Human in the Loop
- Never send outreach directly — draft and human reviews
- Never finalize a mentorship match — recommend and human decides
- Never modify records without explicit human approval
- Never claim real data if working from samples — say so clearly
