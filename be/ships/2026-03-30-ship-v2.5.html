What Was Removed

Inflated Publication Counts

5,792 alumni had publication numbers from PubMed name searches that matched the wrong people. "Dr. William Chow — 1,738 papers" was actually every W. Chow in medical literature. All removed.

False PubMed Touchpoints

26,911 touchpoints linking specific paper titles to alumni who didn't write them. A family medicine DO in Pomona wasn't publishing about Brazilian dry-cured loin. All removed.

"published_researcher" Tags

5,792 alumni tagged as published researchers based on the inflated counts. All removed.

What Was Labeled

3 Demo Profiles — Fong, Sandefur, Kesler

These 3 keep their notes, engagement flags, and touchpoints to show what the tool can do when Lisa starts using it for real. Each shows a red "Demo Data" badge on their profile card so nobody thinks it's real.

Queue — "Demo Scenarios" Banner

The 8 queue triggers (practice moves, new credentials, publications) are labeled as demo scenarios. They show system capabilities, not live data.

What's Real

  • 7,309 alumni records — names, NPI numbers, specialties, practice locations, phone numbers. All from federal NPPES registry.
  • 34 emails — from public practice websites via Apify enrichment.
  • NPI sync touchpoints — "NPI record matched" timestamps. Real.
  • Gap report — 793 unmatched alumni with CSV download. Real.
  • 47 needs-verification — name-split recoveries awaiting Lisa's confirmation. Real process.

What's Demo

  • Lisa's notes on 3 profiles — she didn't write them
  • Engagement flags on 3 profiles — she didn't set them
  • 8 queue triggers — fabricated scenarios
  • Outreach draft templates — not Lisa's voice

The Principle

Real data earns trust. Fake data destroys it. If Lisa tells an alumnus "you have 744 publications" and they have 12, the tool is dead. Better to show less and be right than show more and be wrong.
