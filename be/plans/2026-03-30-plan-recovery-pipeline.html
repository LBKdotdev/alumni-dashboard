The Problem

Lisa gave us 8,166 alumni. We matched 6,283. That leaves 2,289 missing. One of them was Rob — Lisa's husband — which surfaced during a live demo. If Rob is missing, who else is?

What this means

~228 alumni are recoverable right now — they're DOs in NPPES, just missed by the original name matching.

~1,373 alumni are in NPPES but not listed as DO. Could be credential variations (D.O. vs DO vs D.O), dual-credential (MD/DO), or they registered under a different credential. These need manual review or looser matching.

~688 alumni aren't in NPPES at all. Likely retired, deceased, changed careers, never registered an NPI, or significant name changes (marriage). These are genuinely unfindable through the pipeline.

Why They Were Missed

  • Name format mismatch — Lisa's list has "Van Buskirk, John R." but the original pipeline might have searched "VanBuskirk" or split the name wrong
  • Middle name confusion — "Murad Haid, Joan L." has a two-word last name that would break most name parsers
  • Credential filtering — Some DOs register with "D.O.", "D.O", or even just their specialty credential. Strict "DO" matching misses them
  • Duplicate name skipping — 81 duplicate names in the dataset. If the pipeline hit a common name like "John Lee" it may have matched the wrong one or skipped both
  • Recent grads — 820 of the missed are from the 2020s. Some may not have NPI numbers yet

The Plan

Phase 1: Automated Recovery (~228 alumni)

Run all 2,289 missed names against the NPPES API with improved matching:

  • Search by first name + last name (handle multi-word last names)
  • Accept DO, D.O., D.O as valid credentials
  • Use graduation year to disambiguate common names
  • For each match, pull full NPI data (specialty, location, phone) and add to alumni.json
Build a script that runs through all 2,289, matches what it can, and produces a report of what it recovered and what it couldn't.

Phase 2: Credential-Flexible Recovery (~1,373 alumni)

Re-run the missed list without credential filtering — accept any healthcare provider matching the name. Flag these as "credential mismatch — needs review." Lisa or her team confirms which ones are actually WesternU DO graduates who registered under a different credential.

Phase 3: Gap Report for Lisa (~688 alumni)

The truly unfindable. Generate a clean report: "These 688 alumni from your list have no NPI match. They may be retired, deceased, practicing under a different name, or never registered." Give this to Lisa — she'll know which ones matter and can manually provide info for the ones she cares about.
