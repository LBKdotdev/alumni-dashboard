Roadblocks

Blocker

Repo was public. 6,282 real alumni records were exposed.

NPI numbers, emails, practice locations, Dr. Warren's name, outreach drafts, access code (2001), all Be artifacts. Repo is now private as of today. GitHub Pages demo link is dead. Lisa can no longer access the demo.

Blocker

Nothing persists.

Notes, engagement toggles, outreach history, checklists, trigger dismissals — all lost on page refresh. Static site, no backend, no database. Lisa cannot use this as a daily tool. It is demo-only.

Blocker

No hosting while private.

GitHub Pages does not serve private repos on free accounts. We need an alternative host to restore demo access. Cloudflare Pages connects to private repos and is free.

Decisions Needed

Decision 1

Approve Cloudflare as the platform.

Pages (hosting) + Access (auth) + Worker (API) + D1 (database). All free tier. All on one account. scottyledell.us is already on Cloudflare DNS.

Decision 2

Grant Cloudflare account access.

Need login or invite to the Cloudflare account managing scottyledell.us to deploy Pages and Workers.

Decision 3

Pick a demo URL.

Options: alumni-dashboard.pages.dev (free, instant) or alumni.scottyledell.us (custom subdomain, free, your domain). Either works. Custom looks more professional for Lisa.

Email: Temporary Solution (No Backend Needed)

Ready to Ship

mailto: link replaces copy-paste.

When Lisa clicks "Send Email," her default email client (Outlook, Gmail, Apple Mail) opens with To, Subject, and Body pre-filled. She reviews the draft in her own email and clicks Send. Works in demo mode. Works in production. Zero infrastructure.

How it works in the code:

<a href="mailto:dr.sandefur@example.com
  ?subject=Welcome%20to%20Oregon
  &body=Hi%20Dr.%20Sandefur%2C%0A%0AI%20noticed%20you're%20practicing%20in%20Baker%20City...%0A%0AWarm%20regards%2C%0ALisa%20Warren">
  Send Email
</a>
Details

What Lisa sees:

1. Clicks "Draft Outreach" on a trigger or profile.
2. Panel opens with pre-written email in her voice.
3. She edits if she wants.
4. Clicks "Send Email" — her email client opens, ready to send.
5. She hits Send in Outlook/Gmail. Done.

Fallback: "Copy to Clipboard" stays as secondary option.
No email on file: Button disabled, shows "No email on file — enrichment needed."
Limit: mailto: body caps at ~2,000 chars in some browsers. Our drafts are ~500-800 chars. No issue.

Current Approach: Prototype for Lisa

Where We Are

This is a prototype. That's the right positioning.

We're presenting to Lisa, not deploying to production. The goal is to show her what's possible with her alumni data, her voice, and her workflow — and get a "yes, build this for real." Everything we're building is designed to make that conversation happen.

What the prototype already proves:

• Trigger-based outreach works (8 trigger types, real NPI/PubMed signals)
• Her voice can be captured and scaled (dash-free, warm, specific)
• 6,282 real records are queryable and useful
• Enrichment adds real value (34 emails, 54 websites, Google ratings)
• The UX matches institutional expectations (not startup, not generic CRM)

What we can add before the demo (no backend):

• mailto: email — Lisa clicks Send, her email client opens. Real sending, not copy-paste.
• Lisa's notes pre-populated — 3 profiles with her words already in the system. "This was built for me."
• CSV export — "Here's your Vegas alumni list." She downloads it.
• Outreach stats on Dashboard — Frames the tool as measurable.

What we tell Lisa:

"This is a working prototype built on your real alumni data. The trigger queue, the outreach drafts, the enrichment — that's all real. What you're seeing today is what the tool does. What we're building next is making it permanent — your notes save, your outreach tracks, and it lives behind a real login on your own URL. That's a few days of work once you say go."
