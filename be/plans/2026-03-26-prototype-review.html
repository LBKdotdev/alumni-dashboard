Issues Identified & Resolved
Issue #1 — Resolved

PubMed publication counts were inflated

Problem: Directory cards showed "Published 7,896 peer-reviewed papers" and even "350,828 papers." These were PubMed name-search result counts, not authored papers. Lisa would spot it immediately.

Fix: sanitizeNotable() helper strips all specific counts at the rendering layer. Displays "Published researcher (PubMed-indexed)" instead. Signal preserved, misleading number gone. Raw data kept for future author disambiguation.

Issue #2 — Resolved

Projects view needed positioning

Problem: Projects appeared organically, wasn't in original build spec. Needed a decision: keep it or tuck it away.

Fix: Decision: keep it, enhance it. Added enrichment filter (All / Has Email / Has Website / Has Google Rating / No Enrichment), sort by enrichment density, inline badges on alumni cards. Dean vs. Staff checklists with role-aware visibility. The Vegas project with 168 NV alumni is the enrichment showcase. It earns its nav slot.

Issue #3 — Resolved

Outreach draft formatting

Problem: Message body appeared to run together without paragraph breaks. "Warm regards" needed its own line.

Fix: Verified textarea preserves \n natively. Double newlines create visible paragraph gaps. "Warm regards," renders on its own line. Additionally: all 8 trigger drafts and project templates rewritten dash-free. No em dashes, no en dashes. Natural comma and period punctuation. Reads human, not AI.

Issue #4 — Resolved

Meeting transcription quote in SOAP card

Problem: "I was in your shoes three years ago" blockquote was a direct meeting transcription quote. Could feel presumptuous or creepy in a prototype demo.

Fix: Removed the blockquote entirely. SOAP card retains its gold border, the 14-of-15 statistic, three named mentors, Marion Safawi credit. Emotional weight preserved through data, not quotes.

Issue #5 — Resolved

Developer language visible to end users

Problem: "Data Enrichment / Apify Pipeline" and "scraping" appeared in the UI. Not appropriate for a Dean to see.

Fix: Renamed to "Practice Intelligence Layer" throughout. Dashboard, profile, directory all use professional framing. Zero developer jargon visible to Lisa or the board.

Issue #6 — Resolved

"Good morning, Dr. Warren" wasted prime real estate

Problem: The Queue hero banner was a static greeting card. Direct meeting transcription quote. Wasted the most valuable screen real estate.

Fix: Replaced with dynamic mission briefing: most urgent item (overdue follow-ups surface first), high-priority count, nearest project deadline. Dark gradient hero with gold date, white headline, animated pulse line. Changes every day. Operational intel, not decoration.

Enhancements Shipped Post-Review
Enhancement #1

Login Gate

Access code screen (code: 2001) protects alumni PII before any data loads. Mobile: tactile numeric keypad with PIN dots, gold flash on success, shake animation on error. Desktop: focused text input with auto-submit on 4th digit. Session-based auth persists through refresh, clears on tab close.

Enhancement #2

Visual Overhaul

Dark sidebar (gray-900) with burgundy gradient active states and gold left-border accents. Stronger card hover shadows with burgundy tint. Dark mobile bottom nav matching the sidebar. Brand colors activated throughout without losing the calm institutional feel.

Enhancement #3

Dash-Free Draft Engine

Every outreach template (8 triggers + project drafts) rewritten to eliminate em and en dashes. Commas, periods, and natural sentence breaks throughout. Em dashes are a tell-tale AI writing pattern. Removing them makes every draft read like Lisa wrote it.
