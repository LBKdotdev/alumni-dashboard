~U: PubMed Count Sanitization

Strip inflated name-search counts, show qualitative label

What Was Updated

The Problem

5,795 alumni records had PubMed "publication counts" that were actually name-search result counts, not authored papers. Median count was 336. Maximum was 350,828. These would destroy trust instantly if Lisa saw "Dr. Khan: Published 24,767 papers."

The Fix

Added sanitizeNotable() helper that intercepts PubMed count strings at the rendering layer. Specific counts are replaced with a qualitative label. The data file is untouched (preserving the raw NPI-matched search counts for future author disambiguation).

Decisions Made

Why rendering layer, not data fix?

The raw PubMed search counts in alumni.json have future value: when author disambiguation is built (matching on ORCID or co-author networks), the original counts provide a baseline. Fix at display, preserve at source.

Why qualitative, not capped?

Even "Published 12 papers" could be wrong for a common name. A cap (e.g., show if under 50) still risks inaccuracy. "Published researcher (PubMed-indexed)" is always true: the name does appear in PubMed. Zero trust risk.
